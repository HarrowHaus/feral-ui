# feral/ui brand identity

## One-line identity

Controlled-variance components for interfaces with teeth.

## Public category

**Controlled Variance UI**

Not “post-neobrut.” Not “a skin.” Not “more colors.” The public category is controlled variance: a UI grammar where visual instability is permitted only through named axes.

## Brand personality

Cartoon brass knuckles with adult supervision.

The product can be rude, funny, self-mocking, and maximal. The implementation must be clear, accessible, and honest.

## Voice rules

### Do

- Make fun of fake-polished SaaS language.
- Make fun of ourselves.
- Use plain labels for real functionality.
- Be direct when something is not built yet.
- Keep docs clean even when examples are loud.
- Let the humor punch upward: against beige sameness, fake polish, fake authority, and design-system theater.

### Do not

- Do not use fake testimonials.
- Do not claim fake GitHub stars.
- Do not claim accessibility not present in code.
- Do not invent install commands.
- Do not bury functionality under jokes.
- Do not sound like an AI moodboard trying to sell a lifestyle beverage.

## Approved copy examples

- “The visual language is rude. The DOM is not.”
- “No fake CLI cult.”
- “Copy files, no ceremony.”
- “Random chaos is just a clown car with CSS.”
- “Receipts or riot.”
- “We are loud, not stupid.”
- “Registry later. Receipts now.”
- “This component is wearing a traffic cone, but it still has a label.”

## Banned copy examples

- “Loved by thousands of developers” until true.
- “Install with `npx shadcn add feral`” until true.
- “Radix underneath” unless the component imports Radix.
- “Accessible by default” unless the implementation proves it.
- “A revolutionary new paradigm” unless we want to sound like a conference badge.

## Self-roast rule

Self-roast is allowed as long as it increases trust:

Good:
> “The registry folder is parked, not marketed. It becomes real only after component files stabilize.”

Bad:
> “This is visual violence and you should use it on client work.”

## Fake quote handling

Fake quotes can exist only when explicitly labeled as fake, for example:

> Things nobody said yet because we have not earned testimonials.

That keeps the joke and avoids dishonest social proof.
